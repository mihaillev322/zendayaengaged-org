Methodology: how we evaluate relationship status claims

When assessing zendaya relationship status claims, zendaya romance updates, or celebrity wedding news, we apply a consistent set of criteria derived from traditional newsroom standards and fact-checking best practices.

Primary statements: We prioritize direct statements from the individual, their official representatives, or verified social media accounts. A primary statement includes clear language, a date, and context that leaves little room for misinterpretation.

Reputable outlets: We cross-reference reports from major news organizations with established editorial standards, corrections policies, and named editorial leadership. Outlets that employ fact-checkers, cite sources, and issue timely corrections earn higher weight in our evaluation.

Consistency across sources: A single anonymous tip or unsourced headline does not constitute confirmation. We look for corroboration across multiple independent outlets, ideally with overlapping but not identical sourcing.

Anonymous sourcing: We treat anonymously sourced claims as preliminary. Anonymous sources can be legitimate in investigative journalism, but in celebrity relationship reporting, they often reflect speculation, promotional strategy, or misunderstanding. We note when a claim rests solely on anonymous sourcing and do not present it as confirmed.

Photo-only stories: Event photos, red-carpet images, and paparazzi shots are valuable context, but they are not confirmation of engagement or wedding plans. Rings can be styled for fashion, borrowed for events, or misidentified by caption writers. We use photo evidence only when accompanied by corroborating statements or reporting.

Core values

Evidence over speculation: We distinguish clearly between confirmed facts, reputable reporting, and unverified claims. We do not present rumors as facts or use hedging language to obscure weak sourcing.

Transparency: We cite sources, explain our reasoning, and publish corrections when we err. Readers should be able to assess our work and reach their own conclusions.

Respect for privacy: We cover only publicly available information and do not seek, publish, or link to private data, non-public schedules, or invasive content.

International accessibility: We write in clear, neutral English suitable for a global audience. We avoid idioms, cultural assumptions, and region-specific references that might confuse or exclude readers.

No commercial agenda: We do not accept payment for coverage, promote products or services, or alter editorial decisions based on traffic or revenue considerations. This site exists to inform, not to sell.
